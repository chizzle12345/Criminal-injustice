Introduction

Systemic racism in the criminal justice system continues to create long-term disadvantages for Black Americans in the United States. From policing to incarceration, policies have reinforced racial inequality across generations. This project investigates how structural injustice in the system disproportionately impacts Black communities and why it matters today.

Thesis: Through the use of rhetorical strategies like logos, ethos, and pathos, we can analyze how systemic racism is perpetuated in the criminal justice system and how it reproduces economic, political, and social inequalities for Black Americans.

Rhetorical Analysis

This website employs rhetorical appeals:

  • Ethos: Credible sources from peer-reviewed journals establish authority.
  • Pathos: Visuals and personal stories appeal to the audience’s emotions.
  • Logos: Data and research findings provide logical arguments for change.

The message is reinforced through visual imagery, emotional testimony, and academic studies, highlighting how racism is embedded within institutional processes.

Evidence and Research

Bruce & Pettit (2020): Offers a historical overview of racist policies in law enforcement and sentencing, supporting the systemic nature of injustice.

Turner & Jones (2019): Provide empirical research on racial profiling’s impact on Black communities, showing how disparities are enforced daily.

Pager (2003): Demonstrates how Black men with criminal records face long-term employment discrimination, even more than White men with similar records.

Larger Issue Connection

Systemic racism in the justice system also affects housing, education, and economic mobility. Criminal records restrict access to jobs, voting rights, and public benefits, creating a ripple effect across generations.

Explore more: The Sentencing Project

Conclusion

By critically examining the criminal justice system through rhetorical analysis, we expose how racism is perpetuated through policy and practice. This research invites continued dialogue, activism, and legislative reform.
